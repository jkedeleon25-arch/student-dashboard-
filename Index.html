📁 Transformation of Trigonometric Functions

Transformation of Trigonometric Functions

1. Integrals Involving Powers of SINE AND COSINE

Case 1.

Integrals of the form: \( \int \sin^n u \, du \) or \( \int \cos^n u \, du \)

where n is any positive ODD integer, use \( \sin^2 u + \cos^2 u = 1 \).

Examples:

1. \( \int \sin^3 3x \, dx = -\frac{1}{3} \cos 3x + \frac{1}{9} \cos^3 3x + C \)

Case 2.

Integrals of the form: \( \int \sin^n u \, du \) or \( \int \cos^n u \, du \)

where n is any positive EVEN integer, use:

\( \sin^2 u = \frac{1}{2}(1 - \cos 2u) \) or \( \cos^2 u = \frac{1}{2}(1 + \cos 2u) \)

Examples:

1. \( \int \sin^4(1-2x) \, dx = \frac{3}{8} x + \frac{1}{8} \sin(2-4x) - \frac{1}{64} \sin(4-8x) + C \)

2. \( \int \cos^4 2x \, dx = \frac{3}{8} x + \frac{1}{8} \sin 4x + \frac{1}{64} \sin 8x + C \)

Case 3.

Integrals of the form \( \int \sin^n u \cdot \cos^m u \, du \) where at least one exponent is ODD. Use method similar to Case 1.

Examples:

1. \( \int \sin^3 3\phi \cdot \cos^2 3\phi \, d\phi = -\frac{1}{9} \cos^3 3\phi + \frac{1}{15} \cos^5 3\phi + C \)

Case 4.

Integrals of the form \( \int \sin^n u \cdot \cos^m u \, du \) where both exponents are EVEN. Use method similar to Case 2.

Example:

1. \( \int \sin^2 2x \cos^2 2x \, dx = \frac{1}{8} x - \frac{1}{64} \sin 8x + C \)

Additional Case. Products of Sine and Cosine

Use transformations:

\( \sin A \sin B = \frac{1}{2}[-\cos(A+B) + \cos(A-B)] \)

\( \cos A \cos B = \frac{1}{2}[\cos(A+B) + \cos(A-B)] \)

\( \sin A \cos B = \frac{1}{2}[\sin(A+B) + \sin(A-B)] \)

Examples:

1. \( \int \sin 4x \sin 7x \, dx = -\frac{1}{22} \sin 11x + \frac{1}{6} \sin 3x + C \)

2. \( \int \cos 3y \cos 4y \, dy = \frac{1}{14} \sin 7y + \frac{1}{2} \sin y + C \)

3. \( \int \sin 2z \cos 4z \, dz = -\frac{1}{12} \cos 6z + \frac{1}{4} \cos 2z + C \)

2. Integrals Involving Powers of TANGENT and SECANT or COTANGENT and COSECANT

\( \int \sec^2 u \, du = \tan u + c \)

\( \int \sec u \tan u \, du = \sec u + c \)

\( \int \csc^2 u \, du = -\cot u + c \)

\( \int \csc u \cot u \, du = -\csc u + c \)

Case 1.

Integrals of \( \tan^n u \) or \( \cot^n u \), positive integers:

1. \( \int \tan^4 2x \, dx = \frac{1}{15} \tan^5 3x + \frac{1}{9} \tan^3 3x + C \)

Case 2.

Integrals of \( \sec^n u \) or \( \csc^n u \), positive EVEN integers:

1. \( \int \sec^6 x \, dx = \tan x + \frac{2}{3} \tan^3 x + \frac{1}{5} \tan^5 x + C \)

Case 3.

Integrals of \( \tan^m u \cdot \sec^n u \) or \( \cot^m u \cdot \csc^n u \), n EVEN. Use \( \sec^2 u \) or \( \csc^2 u \).

1. \( \int \cot^5 \frac{x}{2} \csc^4 \frac{x}{2} \, dx = -\frac{1}{8} \cot^8 \frac{x}{2} - \frac{1}{6} \cot^6 \frac{x}{2} + C \)

2. \( \int \tan^2 3x \cdot \sec^4 3x \, dx = \frac{1}{15} \tan^5 3x + \frac{1}{9} \tan^3 3x + C \)

Case 4.

Integrals of \( \tan^m u \cdot \sec^n u \) or \( \cot^m u \cdot \csc^n u \), m ODD. Produce \( \sec u \tan u \) or \( \csc u \cot u \).

1. \( \int \sec^3 2x \tan^3 2x \, dx = \frac{1}{10} \sec^5 2x - \frac{1}{6} \sec^3 2x + C \)

2. \( \int \csc^5 5\theta \cdot \cot^3 5\theta \, d\theta = -\frac{1}{30} \csc^6 5\theta + \frac{1}{20} \csc^4 5\theta + C \)
